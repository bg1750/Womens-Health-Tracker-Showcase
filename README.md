# MyRoutine — Women's Health Tracker

> The source code for this project is private. This page exists to share the concept, motivation, and scope of the work.

---

## What It Is

MyRoutine is a personal health tracking interface designed specifically around the female hormonal cycle. It gives women a single place to log their cycle, workouts, nutrition, symptoms, mood, and energy — and to revisit that data over time to find patterns that actually reflect how their body works.

---

## Why I Built It

Women face a set of physical variables that most health tools simply aren't built around. Our hormonal cycle plays out over four weeks — Menstrual, Follicular, Ovulation, and Luteal — and each phase affects weight, strength, appetite, mood, and recovery in ways that are real and measurable. Men's testosterone cycles daily. Ours cycle monthly. Most fitness and nutrition tools are designed with the male physiology as the default.

I've been a lifelong athlete and Olympic Weightlifter. When you compete in weight classes, tracking your body isn't optional — it's part of training. I trained primarily with men, on the same programs, and I noticed early on that there were entire weeks where I simply couldn't hit the same percentages I had hit before, while my training partners could. I noticed foods I couldn't tolerate at certain points in my cycle. I watched my weight shift 5–6 lbs between a follicular week and a luteal one — not from anything I ate or didn't eat, but purely from hormones.

The problem isn't the fluctuation — it's the lack of a frame for it. If you weigh yourself during your follicular phase and feel great, then weigh yourself again a week into luteal and you're up 3 lbs, it's easy to think your plan is failing. It isn't. You're comparing two different physiological states. The right comparison is follicular to follicular, luteal to luteal — month over month.

I built MyRoutine because I wanted a tool that treats the female cycle as the baseline, not the exception. One that helps women stop measuring themselves against a standard that was never built for them, and start building a picture of their own patterns over time.

---

## Vision

The long-term goal is for the interface to learn with the user. The more data entered — meals, workouts, weight, symptoms, energy — the more the app can surface useful patterns and suggestions. If a user consistently logs stomach discomfort after eating tomato sauce in the days before ovulation, the app should eventually flag that and suggest avoiding it around that time.

Apps like Natural Cycles already do this for cycle prediction. MyRoutine aims to extend that logic into nutrition, training, and recovery — a full-picture tool, not just a period tracker.

## What the App Tracks

- **Cycle** — period dates, flow, symptoms, mood, energy, phase detection, and next-period prediction
- **Workouts** — type, duration, intensity, how you felt, individual exercises with sets and reps, and which cycle day the workout fell on
- **Nutrition** — meals by type, calories, macros, and daily water intake
- **Dashboard** — a unified view of all metrics with phase-aware insights and quick-log shortcuts
