# Women's Health Tracker

> The source code for this project is private. This page exists to share the concept, motivation, and scope of the work.

---

## What It Is

My interface is a personal health tracking interface designed specifically around the female hormonal cycle. It gives women a single place to log their cycle, workouts, nutrition, symptoms, mood, and energy — and to revisit that data over time to find patterns that actually reflect how their body works.

---

## Why I Built It

Women face a set of physical variables that most health tools simply aren't built around. Our hormonal cycle plays out over four weeks: Menstrual, Follicular, Ovulation, and Luteal and each phase affects weight, strength, appetite, mood, and recovery in ways that are real and measurable. Men's testosterone cycles daily. Ours cycle monthly. Most fitness and nutrition tools are designed with the male physiology as the default.

I've been a lifelong athlete and Olympic Weightlifter. When you compete in weight classes, tracking your body isn't optional, it's part of training! I trained primarily with men, on the same programs, and I noticed early on that there were entire weeks where I simply couldn't hit the same percentages I had hit before, while my training partners could. I noticed foods I couldn't tolerate at certain points in my cycle. I watched my weight shift 5–6 lbs between a follicular week and a luteal one, not from anything I ate or didn't eat, but purely from hormones.

The problem isn't the fluctuation, its natural and unavoidable. The problem is the lack attention and frame for it. If you weigh yourself during your follicular phase and feel great, then weigh yourself again a week into luteal and you're up 5 lbs, it's easy to think your plan is failing. It isn't. You're comparing two different physiological states. The right comparison is follicular to follicular, luteal to luteal, month over month. This kind of attention and tracking is not the traditional way that individuales go about when they think of a nutrition and training journey.  

I am building this interface because I wanted a tool that treats the female cycle as the baseline, not the exception. One that helps women stop measuring themselves against a standard that was never built for them, and start building a picture of their own patterns over time.

---

## My Vision

The long-term goal is for the interface to learn with the user. The more data entered (meals, workouts, weight, symptoms, energy), the more the app can surface useful patterns and suggestions. For a small example, if a user consistently logs stomach discomfort after eating tomato sauce in the days before ovulation, the app should eventually flag that and suggest avoiding it around that time. Overtime the interface would have an impressive amount of suggestive patterns for nutrition and recovery, overal aiming to ease the stress and obstacles that simply come with being an amazingly strong woman in our world today!

Apps like Natural Cycles already do this for cycle prediction. This interface aims to extend that logic into nutrition, training, and recovery — a full-picture tool, not just a period tracker. It would be ideal to integrate with existing platforms via their APIs: Strong for workout history, MyFitnessPal for nutrition, Natural Cycles for cycle data, and wearables like Oura Ring for sleep and recovery. Allowing the app to work with data users are already logging rather than asking them to start from scratch would greatly enhance the accuracy and effectiveness of the interface.

## What the App Tracks

- **Cycle** — period dates, flow, symptoms, mood, energy, phase detection, and next-period prediction
- **Workouts** — type, duration, intensity, how you felt, individual exercises with sets and reps, and which cycle day the workout fell on
- **Nutrition** — meals by type, calories, macros, and daily water intake
- **Dashboard** — a unified view of all metrics with phase-aware insights and quick-log shortcuts
